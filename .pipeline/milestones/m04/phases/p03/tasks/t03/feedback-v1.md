{"verdict":"needs_revision","feedback":"The onKeyDown passthrough logic is broken due to incorrect JSX prop spread ordering, and the accompanying design deviation explanation is factually wrong.","issues":[{"category":"correctness","description":"In `search-input.tsx` (section 3.3), `{...props}` is spread AFTER `onKeyDown={handleKeyDown}` on the `<input>` element. In JSX, the LAST prop wins when there are duplicates. Since `onKeyDown` is NOT destructured from `...props`, a consumer-provided `onKeyDown` will be in the spread and will OVERRIDE the internal `handleKeyDown` — meaning `onSearch` would silently never fire. Deviation 2 claims the opposite ('our `onKeyDown={handleKeyDown}` on the `<input>` takes precedence (last prop wins in JSX)') but this is factually incorrect because `{...props}` comes after, not before. Fix: destructure `onKeyDown` from the component props (alongside `onChange`, `onSearch`, `onClear`, etc.) and invoke it inside `handleKeyDown`, OR move `{...props}` before the explicit `onKeyDown={handleKeyDown}` prop so the explicit handler takes precedence."}]}
